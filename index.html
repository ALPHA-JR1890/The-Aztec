Aztec Civilization Timeline

Trace the rise and fall of the great Aztec Empire through key historical events.

1325 - Foundation of Tenochtitlan

The Mexica people, having journeyed for generations, founded Tenochtitlan on an island in Lake Texcoco. According to legend, they chose this location after seeing an eagle perched on a cactus, a sign from Huitzilopochtli.

1375 - Early Growth

Tenochtitlan begins to expand and flourish. The city develops agriculture using chinampas, establishing itself as a regional power. Trade networks begin to extend across the valley of Mexico.

1428-1440 - The Triple Alliance

Tenochtitlan, Texcoco, and Tlacopan form a powerful alliance. Together they conquer neighboring city-states and establish the Aztec Empire. Tribute flows in from across Mesoamerica.

1440-1468 - Reign of Montezuma I

Often considered the greatest Aztec ruler, Montezuma I expands the empire to its greatest territorial extent. The city reaches its cultural and architectural peak with magnificent temples, palaces, and public works.

1502-1520 - Reign of Montezuma II

Montezuma II becomes emperor and encounters Spanish explorers led by Hernán Cortés. The empire enters a period of crisis as internal strife and the spread of smallpox weaken Aztec society.

1521 - Fall of Tenochtitlan

After a 93-day siege, the Spanish conquistadors and their indigenous allies conquer Tenochtitlan. The last Aztec emperor, Cuauhtémoc, surrenders. The Aztec Empire officially falls, though its cultural legacy endures.
